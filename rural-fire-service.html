NSW RFS
NSW Rural Fire Service Volunteer Fire Service

The NSW Rural Fire Service (NSW RFS) is the world‚Äôs largest volunteer fire service and the lead combat agency for bush and grass fires in New South Wales. It protects communities, property and the environment across around 95% of the state.

At a Glance
  • Origins over 100 years ago; formal structure under the Rural Fires Act 1997.
  • Focus on rural and regional NSW, including high bushfire-risk areas.
  • More than 70,000 volunteers across 2,000+ brigades.
  • Headquarters in Sydney with regional offices throughout NSW.
Core Responsibilities
  • Bush and grass fires and large campaign fires.
  • Structure fires in rural districts.
  • Storm and flood assistance and damage mitigation.
  • Motor vehicle accidents and search & rescue support.
  • Community education and bushfire mitigation programs.
Prevention & Preparedness
  • Hazard-reduction burns to manage fuel loads.
  • Bushfire risk assessments and local planning.
  • Community engagement such as Get Ready Weekend.
  • Fire trail maintenance and access planning.
Volunteer Culture & Mission

Volunteers receive training in firefighting, incident management, first aid and communications, and play a key role in community resilience and education.

Vision: To provide a world standard of excellence in volunteer-based community fire and emergency services.
Mission: To protect the community and environment by minimising the impact of fire and other emergencies.

Code Summary

Red Highest priority. Require urgent assistance or priority access to radio channels.

Blue High priority. Incident message / incident update.
Green Medium priority. Incident stop / final summary.
Yellow Low priority. Non-incident message or routine traffic.
